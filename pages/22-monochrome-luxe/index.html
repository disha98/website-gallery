Our Philosophy

Where Discipline Meets Artistry

Movement is the purest form of human expression. At Studio Aura, we cultivate an environment where discipline meets artistry, where the body becomes an instrument of emotion, and where every dancer—from the absolute beginner to the seasoned performer—finds their most authentic self through the language of motion.

What We Teach

Forms & Disciplines

  • Ballet

    Classical technique rooted in centuries of tradition. Our ballet program emphasizes proper alignment, turnout, port de bras, and the poetic precision that defines this foundational art form. Pointe work available for advanced students.

  • Contemporary

    Fluid, expressive movement that merges ballet foundations with modern release techniques. Explore floorwork, improvisation, and emotional storytelling through the body.

  • Hip-Hop

    High-energy urban choreography spanning popping, locking, breaking, and commercial styles. Build rhythm, isolations, and stage presence in an electrifying atmosphere.

  • Jazz

    Dynamic and versatile, our jazz classes blend Broadway tradition with contemporary flair. Develop sharp technique, musicality, and performance quality.

  • Salsa

    Partner and solo salsa rooted in Afro-Cuban rhythms. Learn footwork patterns, body movement, and the social connection that makes salsa one of the world's most joyful dances.

  • Bollywood

    Vibrant choreography inspired by Indian cinema. A fusion of classical Indian dance, folk traditions, and modern movement set to infectious Bollywood soundtracks.

  • Zumba

    A dance fitness party that blends Latin and international rhythms with easy-to-follow choreography. High calorie burn, maximum fun, no dance experience required.

  • Yoga

    Vinyasa and Hatha yoga classes designed to complement your dance training. Build flexibility, core strength, breath control, and mental focus on the mat.

  • Gymnastics

    Foundational tumbling, flexibility, and acrobatic technique. Ideal for dancers seeking to expand their physical vocabulary with flips, aerials, and controlled inversions.

Common Questions

FAQs

Do I need prior dance experience to join?

Not at all. We offer classes at every level, from absolute beginner to advanced. Our instructors tailor their guidance so that newcomers feel welcomed and challenged dancers continue to grow.

What should I wear to class?

Comfortable, form-fitting clothing that allows a full range of movement. Ballet classes require ballet slippers and appropriate attire. Hip-hop and jazz classes are best in sneakers. Yoga classes are barefoot.

How do I register for a class?

You can register online through our booking system, call us directly at (555) 234-8901, or simply walk in. We recommend booking in advance for popular evening and weekend sessions.

What are your membership options?

We offer single drop-in classes, 10-class packs, monthly unlimited memberships, and annual memberships. Private lessons and group bookings are also available. Contact us for current pricing.

Is there parking available?

Yes. We have a dedicated parking lot behind the building with 30 spaces. Street parking is also available along Movement Lane. The Arts District metro stop is a five-minute walk away.

Do you offer classes for children?

Yes. Our youth program runs Saturday mornings with classes in ballet, hip-hop, and gymnastics for ages 5 through 16. We also offer a summer intensive camp each July.
